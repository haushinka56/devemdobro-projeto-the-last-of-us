"The Last of Us" is a dystopian series from HBO based on the video game franchise of the same name created by Neil Druckmann. The drama depicts a pandemic-ravaged future that has been devastating to humanity, leaving humans on the brink of extinction.

In the world of "The Last of Us," a deadly virus has swept across the globe, decimating populations and causing widespread chaos and destruction. Society has collapsed, and those who are left must fight for survival in a harsh and unforgiving landscape.

The story follows the journey of Joel and Ellie, two survivors who must navigate this dangerous world together. Along the way, they encounter various groups and individuals, each with their own agendas and motives.

"The Last of Us" is a gripping and emotionally-charged tale that explores themes of love, loss, and the human condition in the face of extreme adversity. It is a powerful reminder of the resilience and strength of the human spirit, even in the darkest of times.
